Menstrual Health and Hygiene

Here are some tips for maintaining menstrual health and hygiene:

  • Change your sanitary pad or tampon every 4-6 hours.
  • Wash your hands before and after handling menstrual products.
  • Avoid using scented products, as they can cause irritation.
  • Practice proper genital hygiene during your period.
  • Dispose of used menstrual products properly.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet to support overall health.
  • Exercise regularly to reduce menstrual cramps and improve mood.

Frequently Asked Questions

Qn 1: What are the common menstrual cycle lengths?

Ans: The average menstrual cycle length ranges from 24 to 38 days, with 28 days being the most common.

Qn 2: How can I track my menstrual cycle?

Ans: You can track your menstrual cycle by noting the start and end dates of your periods and calculating the average cycle length over several months.

Qn 3: Are there any natural remedies for menstrual cramps?

Ans: Yes, some natural remedies for menstrual cramps include applying heat, taking over-the-counter pain relievers, and practicing relaxation techniques.
